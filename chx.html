HISTORY and CULTIVATION of CHOCOLATE

HERE IS A RANDOM QUESTION THAT WILL BE ANSWERED IN THE HISTORY OR CULTIVATION OF CHOCOLATE


HISTORY

HISTORY

The history of chocloate began centuries ago, probably before the pre-Columbian cultures of the Olmecs. Cocoa beans were used as currency and thought of as having magical or divine properties by the Mayans and Aztecs. These ancient cultures mixed a paste made of ground cocoa beans with spices and water to make a chocolate drink which was thought to have magical powers. Chocolate was introduced to the Europeans when they began to conquer the Americas but it wasn't until the middle 1800's that people started to eat it. At first it was considered to be very bitter and rarely eaten. Then a revolution in the art of chocloate making began with the invention of the cocoa press that was able to release the fatty cocoa butter from the cocoa beans. This process allowed a fine powder to be created which could then be mixed with other ingredients and poured into molds to form the edible chocolates we still enjoy today.

CULTIVATION

CULTIVATION

Chocolate comes from a tropical fruit tree typically found in plantations located near the equator. These cocoa trees are planted beneath banana or rubber trees to best replicate their natural envirnment. The trees usually begin producing fruits after the age of five, but their best fruit production occurs 30 to 40 years after maturity and trees often live to be 100 years old. It is from the seeds of the cocoa pod fruit that chocloate is produced. The cocoa beans are taken from the pod, along with the pulp that surrounds the bean and fermented and dried After removal from the branches by hand-cutting, the pods are taken to a central location for opening and removal of the beans and adhering pulp. The beans with pulp are then taken to the fermentary for fermenting and drying. The proper process of fermantion and drying, including maintaining specific temperatures during the processes ensures that the end product of chocolate will have the proper flavor. The beans are then blended, roasted and shelled to form a mass from which choclate is manufactured.
