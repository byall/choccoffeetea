HISTORY and CULTIVATION of TEA

HISTORY

A legend from China says that Emperor Shen Nong who took pride in his knowledge of science discovered tea when one day when leaves from a tree fell into his cup of boiling water. He had ordered his servants, for hygenic reasons, to always boil the water he was served and when he saw the leaves in his water and smelled the fragant aroma of the liguid he decided to try the new drink. He enjoyed it and found it be invigorating. Later, when Japanese Budhist monks visited China they were introduced to the drink and exported it to Japan.

CULTIVATION

The Tea plant is a small shrub with elliptic leaves that needs a warm and humid climate and partial shade to grow. Typically tea plants grow to a height of 10 meters and new growths are harvested from plants aged from 3-4 years. Flowers are also picked to infuse different flavors into teas. Black teas are typically fermented during processing causing them to become more caffeinated then green teas.
