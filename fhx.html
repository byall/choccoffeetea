HISTORY and CULTIVATION of COFFEE

HISTORY

Legend says that a gost herder from Ethiopia originally discovered coffee when his goats became very active and did not want to sleep at night when they ate berries from a certain tree. This sheperd then told the monks in his local abby who discovered that a drink made with the berries would allow them to remain awake through the long hours of their prayers. The monks then spread the knowledge about this new drink and eventually the world all learned about coffee.

CULTIVATION

There are at least two species of coffee, the Arabian and the Robusta which are mainly grown in Africa, Asia and South America. Coffee plants are characterized by persistent diagonal leaves that produce mainly red and purple fruit called coffee cherries. These cheeries contain a green coffee bean enclosed in a silvery skin which can be harvested at 6 to 8 months after flowering for the Arabica plant, and from 9 to 11 months for the Robusta. Once harvested the beans are usually dried by either the dry or humid process. In the dry process, which is used for most coffee processing, the beans are dried in the sun and then ground. The humid method is mostly used to produce the high quality Arabian coffee and invovles the washing of the beans followed the creation of a pulp. That pulp is then fermented, dried and the resultan coffee beans are husked for commercial sale.
